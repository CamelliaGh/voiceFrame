# Data Breach Response Procedures

## Overview

This document outlines the procedures for responding to personal data breaches in accordance with GDPR Article 33 (notification of a personal data breach to the supervisory authority) and Article 34 (communication of a personal data breach to the data subject).

## Breach Definition

A personal data breach is defined as "a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data transmitted, stored or otherwise processed."

### Types of Breaches
1. **Confidentiality Breach**: Unauthorized or accidental disclosure of personal data
2. **Integrity Breach**: Unauthorized or accidental alteration of personal data
3. **Availability Breach**: Unauthorized or accidental loss of access to personal data

## Breach Classification

### Risk Assessment Criteria
- **Nature of Data**: Sensitivity and volume of affected data
- **Number of Data Subjects**: Scale of impact
- **Likelihood of Harm**: Probability of adverse effects
- **Severity of Harm**: Potential impact on data subjects

### Risk Levels
1. **Low Risk**: Minimal impact, contained breach
2. **Medium Risk**: Moderate impact, some data subjects affected
3. **High Risk**: Significant impact, many data subjects affected
4. **Critical Risk**: Severe impact, widespread breach

## Response Team

### Core Team
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**: Overall coordination
- **Security Team Lead**: Technical investigation
- **Legal Counsel**: Legal compliance and notifications
- **Communications Lead**: External communications
- **IT Operations**: System containment and recovery

### Escalation Chain
1. **Level 1**: Security Team Lead
2. **Level 2**: Data Protection Officer
3. **Level 3**: Legal Counsel
4. **Level 4**: Executive Management
5. **Level 5**: Board of Directors (for critical breaches)

## Response Procedures

### Phase 1: Detection and Initial Response (0-2 hours)

#### Immediate Actions
1. **Contain the Breach**
   - Isolate affected systems
   - Disable compromised accounts
   - Preserve evidence
   - Document initial findings

2. **Initial Assessment**
   - Determine breach type and scope
   - Identify affected data subjects
   - Assess potential harm
   - Classify risk level

3. **Internal Notification**
   - Notify DPO immediately
   - Alert security team
   - Inform legal counsel
   - Document all actions

#### Documentation Requirements
- **Incident Report**: Initial breach details
- **Timeline**: Chronological sequence of events
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, logs, system states
- **Actions Taken**: All containment measures

### Phase 2: Investigation and Assessment (2-24 hours)

#### Investigation Activities
1. **Technical Investigation**
   - Analyze system logs
   - Identify attack vectors
   - Determine data accessed
   - Assess system vulnerabilities

2. **Impact Assessment**
   - Quantify affected data subjects
   - Identify data categories involved
   - Assess potential harm to individuals
   - Evaluate business impact

3. **Legal Assessment**
   - Determine notification requirements
   - Assess regulatory obligations
   - Review contractual commitments
   - Identify potential liabilities

#### Documentation Requirements
- **Investigation Report**: Detailed findings
- **Impact Assessment**: Comprehensive impact analysis
- **Risk Evaluation**: Updated risk classification
- **Legal Analysis**: Regulatory and legal implications

### Phase 3: Notification and Communication (24-72 hours)

#### Regulatory Notification (Article 33)
**Timeline**: Within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach

**Required Information**:
- Nature of the personal data breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects
- Categories and approximate number of personal data records
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
- Contact details of the DPO

**Notification Process**:
1. Prepare notification using standard template
2. Review with legal counsel
3. Submit to supervisory authority
4. Document submission and response

#### Data Subject Notification (Article 34)
**Timeline**: Without undue delay if high risk to rights and freedoms

**High-Risk Criteria**:
- Breach likely to result in high risk to rights and freedoms
- No effective technical and organizational measures in place
- Data subjects need to take protective measures

**Notification Content**:
- Clear description of the breach
- Contact details of the DPO
- Description of likely consequences
- Measures taken to address the breach
- Advice on protective measures

**Notification Methods**:
- Direct communication (email, phone)
- Public communication (website notice)
- Media communication (for widespread breaches)

### Phase 4: Remediation and Recovery (Ongoing)

#### Immediate Remediation
1. **Technical Measures**
   - Patch vulnerabilities
   - Strengthen security controls
   - Implement additional monitoring
   - Update incident response procedures

2. **Organizational Measures**
   - Staff training updates
   - Policy revisions
   - Process improvements
   - Access control reviews

#### Long-term Recovery
1. **System Recovery**
   - Restore affected systems
   - Implement enhanced security
   - Conduct security testing
   - Update disaster recovery plans

2. **Business Continuity**
   - Resume normal operations
   - Monitor for related incidents
   - Implement lessons learned
   - Update risk assessments
